# Finding: AV Ecosystem Evidence Boundaries

## Finding

Public ecosystem evidence supports Cosmos as an AV synthetic-data platform, but only some sources directly support Cosmos Transfer usage. Foretellix and Parallel Domain are tied to Cosmos Transfer via NVIDIA Newsroom; Uber is tied to Cosmos platform and DGX Cloud; Waabi and XPENG are Cosmos ecosystem mentions unless further direct evidence is found.

## Evidence

- NVIDIA Newsroom explicitly describes Cosmos Transfer use in the Omniverse Blueprint for autonomous vehicle simulation and mentions Foretellix/Parallel Domain in that context.
- Uber's official release states it will pair Uber trip data with NVIDIA Cosmos and DGX Cloud for AV partners.
- NVIDIA Blog lists XPENG, Uber, and Waabi as working with Cosmos, but does not specify Cosmos-Transfer2.5 workflows for each.

## Impact

- Do not use general partner/adoption statements as proof that a specific Cosmos Transfer setup is production-proven.
- Direct Transfer ecosystem evidence is useful but still not a substitute for validating generated training data.

## Confidence

High for the boundary classification.

## Operational Recommendation

Store company evidence as ecosystem context unless the source explicitly names Cosmos Transfer and the workflow.

## Reuse Trigger

Reread this when citing company adoption or evaluating external credibility.
